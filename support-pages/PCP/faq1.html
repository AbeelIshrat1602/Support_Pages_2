What is primary care, and why is it important?

Primary care serves as the initial point of contact within the healthcare system, offering comprehensive services aimed at maintaining and improving health. It encompasses:

  • Preventive care
  • Diagnosis and treatment of acute and chronic illnesses
  • Health education

Establishing a relationship with a primary care provider (PCP) ensures continuity of care and early detection of potential health issues, contributing to better health outcomes over time.
